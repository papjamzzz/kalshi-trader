KK Trader — User Guide

How the machine works.

Plain English. No jargon. Everything you need to know to run the bot, tune it, and understand what it's doing with your money.

01 — The Big Picture

What is this thing?

KK Trader is a robot that trades prediction markets on Kalshi while you sleep. Kalshi is a legal US exchange where you bet on real-world outcomes — "Will the Fed raise rates?", "Will this movie hit #1?" — and get paid $1 per contract if you're right.

The bot watches hundreds of markets at once, finds ones that look mispriced (meaning: the crowd's estimate seems off), buys in, and sells when it hits your profit target or cuts the loss if it's going wrong.

The core idea: Small edges, applied many times, add up. You don't need to be right 90% of the time. You need to be right more than you're wrong, and let the math work over hundreds of trades.

What every fader does

Every setting is a fader on the left panel. Click the value to type an exact number. Drag the slider to adjust. Hit Apply Settings to save.

04 — The Dashboard

Reading the screen

Transport Bar (top)

The green dot = bot is alive and scanning. The status message tells you exactly what it just did. START turns it on. STOP turns it off (doesn't close open trades). KALSHI ↗ takes you to your account. CHAT opens the AI assistant panel.

Metric Strip (6 numbers)

Daily P&L — your profit or loss today, updates in real time. Green = winning. Red = losing.
Open Positions — how many trades are currently active.
Wins / Losses — closed trades today that hit take profit vs stop loss.
Markets Watched — how many markets KK is scoring this cycle.
Balance — your live Kalshi account balance.

Daily Drawdown Bar

The thin bar under the metrics. Shows how close you are to your Daily Loss Cap. Green = safe. Amber = getting close. Red = almost at the limit. Hits 100% → bot pauses automatically.

Session View (right panel)

Every trade the bot makes appears here as a row, newest at the top. Blue left bar = buy. Green left bar = profitable close. Red left bar = stop loss exit. Filter buttons let you see only wins, only losses, etc.

Open Positions (bottom left)

Shows every currently open trade — the ticker, how many contracts, what side (YES/NO), and entry price. These are live positions on Kalshi right now.

05 — Starting Up

How to run it every day

1
Open KK (the scoring engine)
Double-click ~/kalshi-edge/launch.command in Finder. This starts port 5555 — the brain that scores all the markets.
2
Open KK Trader
Double-click ~/kalshi-trader/launch.command. Browser opens to localhost:5559 automatically.
3
Check your faders
Glance at the settings. Make sure Daily Loss Cap and Max Spend feel right for today. Click any value to type an exact number.
4
Hit START
Green dot lights up. Bot begins scanning. First trades appear in the Session View within the next few scan cycles.
5
Walk away
The machine runs itself. Check back whenever you want. Hit STOP any time to pause — your open positions stay open on Kalshi until they resolve or you close them manually.

Both apps need to be running. KK Trader reads market scores from KK. If KK isn't running, the Trader falls back to fetching directly from Kalshi — which works, but slower.

06 — Glossary

Words you'll see

YES / NOThe two sides of a Kalshi market. Buy YES if you think the event happens. Buy NO if you think it doesn't. Each contract pays $1 if you're right.
ContractsThe unit of a Kalshi trade. 1 contract = you paid X cents. If you're right, you get $1 back. The difference is your profit.
Entry PriceThe price (in cents) the bot paid when it bought. 44¢ means each contract cost $0.44. If it wins, you get $1.00 — profit of $0.56 per contract.
Edge ScoreKK's 0–100 rating of how mispriced a market looks. Higher = more opportunity. Calculated from time remaining, spread width, price distance from 50%, and market category.
Take ProfitThe bot sells when your position gains this %. If you set 20% and a trade gains 20%, it exits and locks in the win.
Stop LossThe bot sells when your position loses this %. Cuts the loss before it gets worse. A controlled small loss is always better than an uncontrolled big one.
Pool VolumeHow many contracts have been traded in a market. Low volume = few participants = easier for insiders to move the price. High volume = many participants = fairer pricing.
Daily Loss CapYour daily hard limit. Once losses hit this, the bot stops for the day. Resets at midnight. The most important risk control you have.
Session ViewThe Ableton-style trade log on the right side. Every buy and sell appears as a clip row. Color-coded: blue = buy, green = win, red = stop hit.
07 — FAQ

Common questions

The bot keeps trading the same market over and over. Is that normal?
Yes, while testing. It means that market keeps passing the filters. Once you raise the Min Edge Score or Min Volume, it gets more selective and spreads trades across different markets.
What if I want to stop trading but keep watching?
Hit STOP. The bot goes idle but the dashboard stays live. Your open positions remain on Kalshi — they'll resolve on their own when the market closes.
How do I know if it's actually making money?
Watch the Daily P&L number at the top left. Green = net profit today. Also look at the Session View — green rows are wins, red rows are stops. Over time you want more green than red, and the wins bigger than the losses.
What happens if my computer sleeps or closes?
The bot stops. It only runs while the app is running on your machine. Your open Kalshi positions stay open (they live on Kalshi's servers), but no new trades will be made and no exits will be triggered until you restart.
How much money should I run this with?
Start small. $50–$100 to prove it works. Watch it for a week. If it's consistently profitable, scale up. The Max Spend fader and Daily Loss Cap are your dials — start conservative and grow into it.
Can I run both KK and KK Trader at the same time?
Yes — that's the intended setup. KK (port 5555) does the market analysis. KK Trader (port 5559) uses that analysis to trade. Run KK first, then KK Trader.
What's the Chat button for?
It opens a panel where you can ask an AI assistant about your trades, positions, and session data. Requires an Anthropic API key in your .env file to work. Once connected, it knows your live P&L and trade history.
08 — Recommended Settings

Where to start

These are safe starting values while you're learning how the machine behaves. Once you've watched it run for a week and it's profitable, you can scale up.

Max Spend: $2.00  ·  Max Positions: 5  ·  Daily Loss Cap: $5.00
Min Volume: 500  ·  Take Profit: 20%  ·  Stop Loss: 15%
Min Edge Score: 75  ·  Scan Interval: 90s

Rule of thumb: If you're seeing too many trades, raise the edge score. If you're seeing zero trades, lower it. The sweet spot is 3–8 trades per day on these settings.
